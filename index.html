I like code. I build interesting things at the Government Digital Service.

Right now, I'm working on GOV.UK, the single website for the government. I've worked on the project since it became a beta. Amongst other things, I've done a lot of work around calendars, local services, and licences.

I'm interested in getting more young people coding. I've worked with Young Rewired State and, last year, The Guardian interviewed me about how I learned to code. I also appeared in Contagious Magazine.

I write things occasionally on Tumblr.

Hacks

  • Anthology
    Anthology is an easier way to keep track of an office or community library. We use it at GDS for our bookshelves.
  • Bowler
    Wrap Foreman to support dependencies and stacks with many processes.
  • hacks.rewiredstate.org
    A database of projects created at Rewired State hack days. Also available on GitHub.
  • Play Your Cards Right
    Play higher or lower with the cost of government websites. Built at National Hack the Government Day 2012.
  • Overbunted
    Can't get enough bunting? Add bunting to every page on GOV.UK
  • Orchestra
    Collect ideas online from a group against the clock. Best in Show at Honda's Power of Minds hack.
  • WhoDoTheyKnow?
    Visualising data released by the government of meetings between ministers and the media. Built at Young Rewired State 2011.
